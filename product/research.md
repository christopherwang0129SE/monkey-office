**Product (working definition):** A 3D-printed desk monkey that measures ambient sound (dB) and physically covers its mouth (hands-over-mouth pose) when noise crosses a configurable threshold. Sold per-desk or per-zone to offices. Companion: dB-logging dashboard for facilities/HR.

**Buyer:** office manager, head of facilities, HR / People & Culture lead, COO of small-to-mid firm, coworking-space operator.

**Why this document exists:** Translate hard research into sales weapons. Every claim below is sourced; the final section ranks the 5 strongest USPs by buyer impact.

---

## TL;DR — the five facts a salesperson should memorize

1. **A single overheard conversation drops productivity up to 66 %** (Banbury & Berry, 2005, cited widely; see Speakwise summary).
2. **Open-plan workers lose 86 minutes per day** to noise and interruptions (Steelcase × IPSOS, 10 500-worker study).
3. **It takes 23 min 15 s to refocus** after an interruption (Gloria Mark, UC Irvine).
4. **60 % of office workers are disrupted by noise at least twice a week** (FacilitiesNet, Oct 2025); only **35 % are satisfied with office noise levels** (Leesman Index, 2024-25).
5. **Distraction accounted for 93.6 % of annual productivity loss** at one large U.S. manufacturer — about **15× the cost of health-related absenteeism**.

Plug these into the ROI math in section 4 and the pitch writes itself.

## 1. Productivity loss from office noise

| Stat | Source |
| --- | --- |
| Up to **66 % productivity drop** when exposed to a single nearby conversation ("irrelevant speech effect") | [Banbury & Berry, 2005 / Speakwise summary](https://speakwiseapp.com/blog/workplace-noise-statistics) |
| **86 minutes/day lost** to noise + interruptions in open-plan | [Steelcase × IPSOS, 10 500 workers](https://www.steelcase.com/eu-en/research/articles/topics/privacy/much-noise/) |
| **23 min 15 s** to fully refocus after an interruption; ~2 intervening tasks before returning | [Gloria Mark et al., UC Irvine](https://ics.uci.edu/~gmark/chi08-mark.pdf) |
| **99 % of workers** say office noise impairs concentration | [Steelcase](https://www.steelcase.com/eu-en/research/articles/topics/privacy/much-noise/) |
| **70 % of open-plan workers** frequently distracted by noise | Steelcase / Workplace Insight |
| **1 %** of employees can block distractions without taking extra steps (down from 20 % in 2015) | [Oxford Economics × Plantronics](https://workinmind.org/2018/08/31/sound-advice-how-acoustics-hold-a-key-to-productivity/) |
| **Brief interruptions can double a worker's error rate** | Steelcase, Restoring Focus & Privacy |
| Noise increases negative mood by **25 %** and sweat response by **34 %** | Speakwise summary of multiple studies |

**Interpretation for pitch:** Even if you discount these by half, the per-employee productivity hit is enormous. A 5 % recapture of 86 min/day = ~22 min/day per person → 1.83 hr/week → ~95 hr/year per employee.

## 2. Health impact — noise as a measurable psychosocial hazard

| Fact | Source |
| --- | --- |
| Noise triggers cortisol release → elevated pulse, blood pressure, vascular inflammation | [Hahad et al., Oxidative Medicine & Cellular Longevity, 2019](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1155/2019/4623109) |
| Systematic review: occupational noise exposure → statistically significant cortisol elevation across industries | [Naderyan Fe'li et al., 2022](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/09574565221139584) |
| WHO Environmental Noise Guidelines link chronic noise to sleep disturbance, cognitive impairment, hearing loss, cardiovascular disease | WHO European Region guidelines |
| Long-term noise → measurable rises in angiotensin-II → hypertension risk | Hahad et al., 2019 |
| Negative effects observed **below permissible OSHA limits** — workplace noise harms even when "legal" | [PMC12044883, 2025 scoping review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12044883/) |

**Why this matters commercially:** Health costs are not abstract. They show up in sick days, EAP claims, private-health-insurance premium creep, and turnover. Sales angle: "the noise harming productivity is also the noise driving your sick-day curve."

## 3. Standards & compliance — why office acoustics are now reportable

### WELL Building Standard v2 (the buyer's framework)

- **Open-plan and lobby spaces: max NC 40** (Noise Criteria)
- **Enclosed offices: max NC 35**
- **Background noise (HVAC, lighting): ≤ 40 dBA**
- Acoustic separation between noisy zones (break rooms, gyms) and quiet zones (meeting rooms, focus areas) is a verified feature

[WELL v2 — Internally generated noise / dbxacoustics summary](https://dbxacoustics.com/acoustic-design-and-the-well-building-standard/)

### ISO 45003 (2021)

- First global standard for **psychological health & safety at work**
- Explicitly names **noise, lighting, and inadequate space** as recognized psychosocial hazards
- Across UK / North America, legal interpretations of duty of care are expanding — regulators now look for **systematic management, not ad-hoc wellness initiatives**

[ISO 45003 overview, NASP](https://www.naspweb.com/blog/psychosocial-hazards/)

**Sales hook:** "Your CSR/ESG report claims you protect employee wellbeing. Where is the dB log?"

## 4. Financial / ROI math (the killer slide)

### Inputs (US benchmark)

- Average knowledge-worker salary: **$111 170/yr ≈ $53/hour** ([Glassdoor / Salary.com](https://www.glassdoor.com/Salaries/knowledge-worker-salary-SRCH_KO0,16.htm))
- Loaded cost (salary × ~1.3 for benefits/overhead): **~$69/hour**
- Working hours per year: ~1 880

### ROI scenarios per employee, per year

| Productivity recovered | Hours regained/yr | Value at $69/hr |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 % | 18.8 | **$1 297** |
| 2.5 % | 47 | **$3 243** |
| 5 % | 94 | **$6 486** |

For perspective: Steelcase's 86 min/day lost is **~17.9 % of an 8-hr workday**. Even a **modest 10 % recapture** (≈9 min/day) is **18.6 hours/yr = $1 280/year per employee**.

### Comparable wellness-product claims

- **Awair / Kaiterra (air quality monitors)** publicly claim up to **$6 500 – $15 500/employee/year** in productivity and cognitive savings via better air ([Awair Office](https://www.getawair.com/solutions/office) / [Kaiterra IAQ resources](https://learn.kaiterra.com/en/resources/how-much-does-iaq-monitoring-cost)). Noise is the **#1 employee complaint** about workplaces — bigger than air. The comparable claim for noise should be at least as large.

### Hard cost of attrition (the "one save = product paid for life" math)

- Replacement cost for high-skill roles: **50-200 % of annual pay; average 150 %** ([Gad Allon, Wharton substack summary](https://gadallon.substack.com/p/the-real-cost-of-knowledge-worker))
- Lost output during ramp: ~$37 500 for a $100k engineer
- One employee saved from quitting due to "noise drove me crazy" → **pays for hundreds of monkeys**

**Pitch math (use on cold calls):**
> "You have 40 people in this office. At even a 2 % productivity recapture from less ambient noise, that's about $130 000/year. The monkey program runs about $X. Break-even week 2."

(Final $X depends on chosen pricing tier — see section 11.)

## 5. Hybrid work — the structural tailwind

- **Coworking market: $12.93 B (2025) → $74.28 B by 2035** at 19.1 % CAGR ([Coherent / Polaris / Next MSC reports](https://www.coherentmarketinsights.com/industry-reports/coworking-spaces-market))
- **28.2 % of full-time employees** now work hybrid; **only 22 %** fully on-site ([Owl Labs / Mordor](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/united-states-co-working-office-space-market))
- **92 % of employees** say community + collaboration are key reasons to come to office — but **only 39 %** agree mandated office days improve productivity ([HBR, "Hybrid Still Isn't Working", July 2025](https://hbr.org/2025/07/hybrid-still-isnt-working))
- "Acoustic privacy is among the top reasons employees resist Return-to-Office mandates" ([citybiz / Office Soundproofing, 2025](https://www.citybiz.co/article/790827/office-soundproofing-gains-attention-as-hybrid-work-evolves/))
- Office pod / sound-booth orders rising sharply with hybrid adoption

**Implication:** RTO is mandated by HQ, but the office is louder than it was pre-COVID (denser layouts, more Zoom calls from desks, less working-from-home option). **The noise problem is structurally worsening.** Best timing in 20 years to sell into it.

## 6. Behavioral / nudge science — why the monkey works where rules don't

- Nudge theory (Thaler & Sunstein, Nobel 2017) — **make the right behavior the easy behavior**
- **Real-time, visible feedback** is the highest-leverage nudge format because it makes hidden behaviors visible (e.g., utility-bill comparison signs cut energy use; smiley-face speed signs cut speeding)
- Workplace nudges outperform classroom training because they **operate in the flow of work**, use spaced repetition, and measure behavior not completion ([infeedo, 2025](https://www.infeedo.ai/blog/how-ai-nudging-actually-changes-workplace-culture-2025-guide))

**Why the monkey is the *right form factor*, not just a feature:**

1. **Externalizes social enforcement.** Nobody wants to shush a colleague — that's the structural reason open-plan noise compounds. The monkey shushes for you, neutrally.
2. **Physical + characterful** (3D-printed monkey) > digital dashboard. A dashboard buried in Slack changes no behavior. A monkey on the desk changes posture in 0.5 seconds.
3. **Anti-confrontational.** It's funny. Visitors comment on it. New hires learn the norm without being told.
4. **Cultural artifact, not a sensor.** Like the Salesforce bell or the Mailchimp office hat — gives the office an identity.

This is the hardest USP for competitors to copy because **the product *is* the form factor**, not the dB sensor.

| **Cambridge Sound / Lencore (sound masking)** | Whole-office acoustic infrastructure — pink noise piped through ceiling speakers | Capex install ($5-15 per sq ft); hides noise rather than reducing it. Different category. | — |

**Gap in the market:** There is **no characterful, personality-led office-noise product**. SoundEar owns the clinical visual-sign category. Yacker Tracker owns the K-6 classroom category. **Nobody owns "the office talisman that happens to monitor sound."**

This is the wedge. The product is **functionally similar to SoundEar but emotionally opposite** — a thing employees *want* on their desk.

## 8. Call / meeting quality angle

- Background noise on Teams/Zoom is the #1 audio quality issue — Microsoft has shipped **AI-based 3-level noise suppression** specifically because the underlying problem is so widespread ([Microsoft Teams blog](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/blog/microsoftteamsblog/reduce-background-noise-in-microsoft-teams-meetings-with-ai-based-noise-suppress/1992318))
- In hybrid offices, meetings are **camera-on, microphone-on, all-day** — the office is no longer "quiet around the calls"; it *is* the calls
- Monkey reduces source noise → fewer "can you repeat that?" moments → shorter, sharper meetings → measurable calendar savings

**Sales angle for sales-floors and customer-facing teams:** "Your reps are on the phone all day. Your prospects can hear your office. The monkey makes your office sound like the firm you want to be."

## 9. Recruiting / employer brand

- Gen Z prioritizes wellness, mental health, and work-life balance more explicitly than any prior cohort ([Georgetown McDonough research](https://msb.georgetown.edu/news-story/research-and-insights/new-research-explores-how-millennials-and-gen-z-are-driving-a-new-definition-of-workplace-wellness/))
- Glassdoor reviews regularly cite "too loud", "couldn't concentrate", "had to wear headphones all day" as reasons-for-1-star
- Visible wellness gestures (plants, standing desks, ergonomic chairs, air monitors) signal cared-for environment to candidates on tour

**The monkey doubles as recruiting collateral.** It's photogenic, postable, and signals "this is a place that takes the *feel* of work seriously". A LinkedIn post showing the monkey covering its mouth during a busy afternoon will outperform 99 % of HR-team employer-brand content. Plan for it.

## 10. Liability / duty of care

- **ISO 45003 (2021)** establishes noise as a documented psychosocial hazard
- UK Health & Safety Executive, EU OSH framework, Australian Code of Practice for Managing Psychosocial Hazards — all increasingly require **evidence of systematic monitoring**, not just policy statements
- Insurance angle (forward-leaning, verify with insurers): documented monitoring = lower psychosocial-claim risk profile → potentially favourable EAP/health insurance terms

**Sales hook for risk-averse buyers (legal, finance, regulated industries):**
> "When a tribunal asks how you managed psychosocial workplace exposure, do you have data — or do you have an opinion?"

The dashboard becomes the audit trail. **Selling the dashboard to legal/HR may be a stronger procurement path than selling the monkey to office managers** in some segments.

## 12. Top 5 USPs — ranked by likely buyer impact

Each USP includes the one-line cold-call opener + the 1-2 facts that back it.

### 🥇 USP #1: "The shush no one resents"
**Opener:** "Your office has one structural problem nobody talks about: nobody wants to shush a colleague. We sell a monkey that does it for you — neutrally, automatically, and people laugh instead of getting offended."

**Backing:** 99 % of workers report noise impairs concentration, but peer-shushing is socially toxic — that's why noise compounds in open-plan. Externalized, data-driven enforcement is the only mechanism that scales without damaging culture. (Steelcase; Banbury & Berry; ISO 45003 nudge-science literature.)

**Why #1:** This is the *only* problem the product uniquely solves. Apps measure. Panels absorb. Pods isolate. **Only the monkey changes behavior in real-time without a human being the bad guy.** This is the moat.

---

### 🥈 USP #2: "Up to 86 minutes a day per person — that's $1 200-6 500/year"
**Opener:** "Open-plan noise costs your team an average of 86 minutes per day per person. At a $69 loaded hourly cost, recapturing even 10 % pays for the entire program in a single week."

**Backing:** Steelcase × IPSOS 10 500-worker study; Banbury & Berry 66 % productivity drop on single conversation; Awair's comparable air-quality claim of $6 500-15 500/employee/year shows the buyer accepts this category of math.

**Why #2:** Quantifies the pain in the buyer's currency (dollars), with conservative scenarios that pass the smell test.

---

### 🥉 USP #3: "Your ESG report claims employee wellbeing. Where's the data?"
**Opener:** "Most CSR reports promise to protect employee wellbeing. Almost none can produce a dB log proving they did. We give you the metric — and the audit trail — for your S-pillar."

**Backing:** WELL v2 sets explicit acoustic thresholds (NC 40 open / NC 35 enclosed / ≤40 dBA background). ISO 45003 (2021) names noise a psychosocial hazard. Regulators across UK / NA / EU are shifting from policy-checking to evidence-checking.

**Why #3:** Targets HR / legal / sustainability buyers who control budget but aren't reachable through "productivity" pitches. Different procurement door, same product.

---

### 4. "Your Zoom calls are louder than your office should be"
**Opener:** "Every call your team takes leaks the sound of your office to your prospects. Microsoft and Zoom shipped AI noise suppression because the underlying problem is unfixable from the cloud. We fix it at source."

**Backing:** 28 % hybrid, 22 % fully on-site (Owl Labs); meetings are camera-on/mic-on all day; Microsoft Teams' triple-level AI suppression is corporate acknowledgment that ambient noise on calls is universal.

**Why #4:** High resonance with sales floors, customer-success teams, professional-services firms — buyers who already feel call quality is a brand issue.

---

### 5. "The office talisman that runs itself"
**Opener:** "Plants, standing desks, ergonomic chairs — every wellness investment is invisible to candidates and visitors. The monkey is on the desk, photogenic, charming, and instantly signals *we take how this place feels seriously*."

**Backing:** Gen Z wellness priorities; Glassdoor noise complaints; recruitability hard-coded into Gen Z hiring decisions; no competitor has a personality-led product in this category.

**Why #5:** Soft USP — converts harder than #1-3 but expands the pitch surface to employer-brand and culture buyers (CHRO, founder-led companies, design-conscious scale-ups).

## 13. What this research can't tell us (open follow-ups for Benjamin)

1. **Willingness-to-pay** — only the market can answer. First 10 cold calls should test €99 / €499 / €999 / per-seat SaaS price points.
2. **Whether SoundEar already owns the procurement-ready segment** in DK/EU. Mystery-shop SoundEar's sales process to find out where their pitch is weak.
3. **Cultural translation** — does the "monkey covers mouth" gesture read the same in DE, FR, US, JP? Check before committing to one figurine.
4. **Hardware cost ceiling** — 3D-print + microphone + servo + microcontroller likely lands €15-35 BOM at low volume. Confirm with a prototype before locking pricing.
5. **Power model** — USB-C plugged in always-on vs. battery? Battery = better for placement freedom but adds charging operations overhead.
6. **Regulatory / privacy framing** — is dB-only logging ever construed as employee surveillance? Probably not (no voice content), but pre-empt the FAQ.
