# Global AI Agent / Automation Platform Competitors — Agent360 Strategic Analysis

*Compiled May 2026. Sources: vendor homepages, vendor pricing pages, 2026 third-party reviews.*
*Lens: Agent360 = Danish chat-based AI automation, "the layer that connects your SaaS — and that you own yourself," 42+ integrations, 8 Nordic connectors (e-conomic, Dinero, etc.), JesperAI voice-agent built in, target solo founders → 200-person teams.*

## 1. PRIMARY COMPETITORS

### 1. Lindy.ai — "Personal AI work assistant"

**Headline (verbatim):** *"Lindy is a personal AI work assistant that runs inbox, meetings, calendar, follow-ups, and ad hoc work across tools."* They actively contrast: *"designed for people who want an assistant that does work across their apps, not just a chatbot that answers questions."*

**Target customer:** Individual knowledge workers, founders, exec assistants, sales/CS leaders. Marketed at "400K+ professionals." Lindy is sold as an *AI employee* per seat, not a workflow tool per ops team.

**Pricing:** No permanent free tier. 7-day free trial (Plus features, no card). Flat monthly seats:
- Plus $49.99/mo (2 inboxes, ~100 integrations)
- Pro $99.99/mo (3 inboxes, 3× usage, computer-use)
- Max $199.99/mo (5 inboxes, 7× usage)
- Enterprise (custom — SSO, SCIM, HIPAA, BAA)

**Top differentiators they market:**
1. iMessage/SMS delegation — text Lindy from your phone like a human EA.
2. Full meeting lifecycle (prep → notes → action items) baked in.
3. "Proactive" — notices what needs attention, doesn't wait to be asked.
4. Outcome-based — you describe goals, not build flows.
5. SOC2 Type II + HIPAA + GDPR with AES-256 (front-loaded to enterprise buyers).

**Integrations:** 100–200 native (Lindy's own "Integrations" page), with reviews citing 4–5k via partner extensions. Standouts: Gmail, Outlook, HubSpot, Salesforce, Slack, Notion.

**Sales motion:** Self-serve PLG with a 7-day trial that auto-converts. Enterprise contact-sales above 5 inboxes. No demo gate on the front door.

**GTM tactics:**
- Heavy YouTube and TikTok presence with sub-2-minute "build an AI employee in X minutes" demos.
- Long-form comparison blog content (`/blog/make-review`, `/blog/langflow-alternatives`) that ranks for competitor names.
- 50+ pre-built template gallery (sales assistant, medical scribe, CS chatbot, executive assistant) — heavy use of *persona-named* templates rather than abstract workflows.
- Founder Flo Crivello posts daily on X — strong founder-led content engine.

**Sales weapons:** Persona-named templates; sub-60-second product demos; "delegate from your phone" as an emotional hook; aggressive SEO targeting "X alternative" pages.

**Weaknesses Agent360 can exploit:**
- *Credit/usage opacity:* Top 2026 user complaint is unpredictable bills — "credits disappear far faster than expected." Free plan is functionally useless (premium actions excluded).
- *English-only*, US-Google-centric (heavy Google permissions).
- *No Nordic accounting integrations* — zero e-conomic, Dinero, Fortnox, Visma.
- *Per-inbox seat model* punishes teams that want shared automation.
- *No voice-agent natively* (voice is "added-on"). Agent360+JesperAI is a single product.
- *Workflow-debuggability* weak on complex multi-step processes.

4. "Single pane" governance — approvals, escalations, RBAC, audit logs.
5. Pre-deployment quality (scenarios, production checks, monitoring) — sold as "agents you can trust."

**Integrations:** Marketing claims "1,000+" on the agents marketplace, "2,000+" listed in enterprise tier feature list.

**Sales motion:** Hybrid — self-serve sandbox to attract builders, but production = sales-led. ~400 agent templates in marketplace. They host an "AI Workforce Summit" series and AWS Marketplace listing for procurement-friendly buyers.

**GTM tactics:**

**Weaknesses Agent360 can exploit:**

## 2. SECONDARY COMPETITORS

- **Pipedream** — Dev-first serverless workflow. Free 100 credits/day; Basic $29/mo (2k/day); Advanced $79/mo (10k/day). 1 credit = 30s @ 256MB compute. *Strength:* code-native (Node/Python). *Weakness:* needs a developer. Not a competitor for non-technical founders.
- **Workato** — Enterprise iPaaS. Entry ~$10–15k/year; enterprise often $50k+. Quote-only. Merlin agent layer. *Strength:* deep enterprise integrations + governance. *Weakness:* totally inaccessible to SMB/founders.
- **Tray.ai** — Enterprise-only, no public pricing. **Merlin Agent Builder** as 2026 flagship. *Strength:* enterprise governance, audit logs, human-in-loop. *Weakness:* sales-led only; SMB hostile.
- **Latitude** — AI agent observability + prompt engineering platform. *Strength:* developer/MLOps tier. *Weakness:* infrastructure tool, not end-user automation.
- **CrewAI** — Code-first multi-agent Python framework. *Strength:* developer ergonomics, multi-agent orchestration. *Weakness:* requires engineering team.
- **Langflow** — Visual drag-and-drop AI agent builder, 140k+ GitHub stars. *Strength:* RAG prototyping speed, open source. *Weakness:* still developer-leaning; not a SaaS-stack connector layer.
- **Flowise** — TypeScript-native open-source visual agent builder, 38k+ stars, AgentFlow V2 with HITL nodes. *Strength:* OSS + multi-agent. *Weakness:* self-hosted ops burden, no out-of-box SaaS connectors at scale.
